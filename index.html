01 — The Proposal

The Proposal

We got engaged in Batanes, specifically at Chamantad-Tiñan Viewpoint in Sabtang in 2024. It was a quiet and intimate moment, with only the breathtaking mountains, endless sea, and blue sky as our witnesses. In a place so beautiful and peaceful, we said yes to forever and began the next chapter of our story.

02 — Why Batanes

The island that decided everything

Batanes doesn't ask to be noticed, it simply is. Rolling hills the color of old brass, stone houses built to outlast typhoons, a silence you can hear. We didn't choose it to impress anyone. We chose it because it already felt like us.

01

Landscape

Sweeping views of the rolling hills of Marlboro Country, the dramatic cliffs of Vayang, stone houses nestled beneath open skies, and the endless coastline where the wind meets the sea.

02

Local Culture

The quiet warmth of the Ivatan people, centuries-old stone-and-cogon houses standing resilient against the elements, and a slower rhythm of life that gently reminded us to be fully present in every moment.

03

Peaceful Atmosphere

No traffic, no city noise, only the steady rhythm of the wind, waves meeting the limestone coast, and the kind of silence that allows you to hear your own thoughts.

04

Rolling Hills

Standing atop these windswept hills, we looked at each other and quietly knew: this was where we wanted to begin the rest of our lives together.

05

Weather & Wind

Batanes is known for its powerful winds and ever-changing weather. Rather than resisting its unpredictability, we learned to embrace it, trusting that every breeze and shifting cloud would become part of our story.

06

Churches

Centuries-old stone churches, weathered by time and the island's relentless winds, provided more than a venue. They lent our ceremony a quiet sense of reverence, history, and enduring faith.

03 — Wedding Vision

Earth tones, and everything we left out

Our mood board wasn't built around extravagance, it was built around restraint. Every fabric, every color, and every bloom was selected to complement the landscape rather than compete with it. We intentionally stayed away from satin sheens, glittering details, and glossy materials, embracing soft linens, matte textures, and warm earth tones that felt as though they belonged in Batanes.

Rustic, at heart

Raw wood, woven textures, and unpolished stone ran through every styling choice, nothing shined that didn't need to. It was less about a "look" and more about letting things feel handmade, lived-in, and warm.

A quiet Bruno Mars thread

Woven gently beneath the earth tones was a nod to the music that means the most to us. Our welcome banner drew its spirit from Bruno Mars' first solo album, Doo-Wops & Hooligans, its warmth, its soul, its era, reimagined in our own words rather than quoting the songs themselves. It never took over the aesthetic, just a few personal touches for anyone who knows to look. More on that later.

04 — Planning Journey

Everything it took to get here

A destination wedding on an island with a few thousand residents and its own weather system is not simple. Here's the long version, the version we wish someone had shown us before we started.

Some venues are chosen because they're beautiful. We chose Batanes because it felt like us. During our engagement in Sabtang, surrounded by the island's quiet grandeur, we realized we weren't simply visiting a place, we were discovering where our next chapter would begin. For over five years, we had dreamed of exchanging vows here, drawn to its simplicity, its serenity, and the way it lets nature speak for itself. Our vision was never about an extravagant celebration. We wanted a wedding that felt honest, intentional, and quietly beautiful. In the end, Batanes wasn't just the backdrop to our wedding, it became part of our story.

We knew from the start that we wanted to work with local Ivatan suppliers. No one understands Batanes better than the people who call it home. Their talent, creativity, and genuine hospitality made every part of our wedding feel authentic. We entrusted everything to Batanes Weddings & Events by IBS as our all-in-one coordinator, and they brought together an amazing team that helped turn our vision into reality.

One of the biggest parts of planning a Batanes wedding is getting the timing right. We first confirmed our wedding date with our coordinator before booking our flights. Since flights to Batanes are limited and can be expensive, planning ahead helped us save money and avoid unnecessary stress. Currently, Philippine Airlines is the only airline offering direct flights to Batanes from Clark, so securing your tickets early is important. If you're traveling with more than nine guests, it's also worth requesting a group booking to make the arrangements more convenient.

For accommodations, we relied on our coordinator's recommendations. They provided several hotel options based on our budget, preferred location, and the needs of our guests, making the planning process much easier.

It's also important to set your budget based on the size of your wedding. Most packages are priced according to the number of guests, whether you're planning an elopement, a 20–30 guest celebration, or a larger wedding. We chose the 30-guest package, which perfectly matched the intimate wedding we envisioned.

When we first visited Batanes, we instantly fell in love with Tukon Chapel. Its stone-house architecture, peaceful atmosphere, and breathtaking views felt so uniquely Batanes. We couldn't picture ourselves getting married anywhere else. For the reception, the choice was just as easy, Naidi Lighthouse. We had always imagined celebrating on a cliff overlooking the sea, with the lighthouse standing nearby, the hills in the distance, and a beautiful Batanes sunset as our backdrop. It was everything we had dreamed of in one place.

One thing we learned is that you can never fully predict the weather in Batanes. We scheduled our wedding during summer to avoid the rainy season, but we also knew that Batanes weather can be unpredictable. Since our reception was outdoors, we prepared an indoor venue as a backup in case the weather didn't cooperate. We hoped for the best but planned for the unexpected. We also realized how important it is to have buffer days before the wedding, especially for destination weddings, to allow room for unexpected weather changes, travel delays, or emergencies.

For a destination wedding, it's important to prepare all legal and church documents early. Always coordinate with your wedding coordinator and ask for the complete requirements before the wedding day. Since the location is far, we made sure everything was arranged ahead of time and considered any possible risks to avoid last-minute problems.

One of the first things we shared with our coordinator was our wedding theme and motif. We chose an earth-toned palette with a rustic, minimalist style because we wanted Batanes to be the true highlight of our wedding. With its breathtaking landscapes, we felt that less decoration meant more intention.

For logistics, we carried our wedding attire with us on the flight for peace of mind, while the rest of our décor, props, and other wedding essentials were shipped to Basco ahead of time. Every wedding is different, so it's best to coordinate closely with your wedding coordinator to plan the safest and most convenient way to transport everything you'll need.

05 — Wedding Week

Five days, one island, a lifetime of memory

A day-by-day journal of the week itself, bookended by a family tour of Batanes before and after the wedding day, scroll sideways to move through it, the way the week itself seemed to move: quickly, and all at once.

Day One

Arrival & Family Tour

After landing in Batanes, we started with an initial tour of the island. Since our wedding was also an adventure, we wanted our family to experience and appreciate the beauty of Batanes before the big day. Seeing everyone excited, amazed, and in awe of the breathtaking landscapes made the experience even more special.

Day Two

Welcome Dinner

Our first dinner after arriving in Batanes was at Little Cafe, conveniently located just beside our hotel. It is a well-loved spot where visitors gather to enjoy good food, beautiful views, and the breathtaking sunset overlooking the ocean. It was the perfect way to begin our wedding week, sharing our first meal together as a family and soaking in the beauty of the island that would soon witness our special day.

Day Three

Wedding Day

It was the best day of our lives. A dream years in the making, finally happening in the place where we always imagined saying our vows. Batanes was more than just a destination for us; it was the place we always envisioned for this moment. We felt truly blessed that God gave us the most beautiful day, not only on our wedding day but throughout our entire stay. Every view, every moment, and every detail felt like a gift we will always remember.

Day Three, Postnup

Golden Hour Portraits

Our postnup session took place at Vayang Rolling Hills during golden hour, when the soft light beautifully covered the landscape. Surrounded by the rolling hills and the breathtaking views of Batanes, it was the perfect setting to capture quiet moments together after saying our vows.

Day Three, Sunset

Reception

Our reception was held at Naidi Lighthouse, a place that perfectly captured the beauty and simplicity we envisioned for our celebration. Before the formal program, we took the time to climb the lighthouse and watch the sunset with our family and guests. It was a meaningful moment of togetherness before we gathered for an intimate evening filled with heartfelt conversations, laughter, and memories with the people who shared this special day with us.

Day Three, Evening

Night of Celebration

We kept our program simple and short because we wanted more time for natural conversations and casual interactions with our family and guests rather than a celebration that felt too structured. One of the most memorable and unique moments of the night was performing the Fundango Ivatan, a traditional Ivatan folk dance that celebrates joy, unity, and love, making it the perfect way to honor the culture of Batanes and our wedding celebration.

Day Four

Family Tour & Farewell

The day after our wedding, we spent our final moments in Batanes exploring Sabtang and taking in more of the island's beauty with our family. We ended our journey by watching the sunset at Vayang Rolling Hills, a quiet and beautiful farewell to a place that gave us memories we will carry forever.

06 — Personal Touches

The details that were just for us

Not everything on this island belonged to Batanes alone. A few details belonged only to us, quiet references that most guests walked past, and that we hope you'll enjoy finding.

The welcome banner

Our welcome banner was a small nod to the artist who brought us together: Bruno Mars. Inspired by the fun, warmth, and playful energy of his first solo album, Doo-Wops & Hooligans, we wanted it to have a little bit of character and showmanship while still blending with our earth-toned wedding palette. It was a simple detail that carried a meaningful piece of our story into the celebration.

The photo backdrop wall

Instead of having a traditional photo booth, we chose to let Batanes create the perfect backdrop for our guests. The cliffside by the sea, with the lighthouse standing beautifully in the distance, became the natural setting for their photos. We wanted the experience to feel authentic to the place, allowing everyone to capture memories with the breathtaking beauty of Batanes as the background.

The song behind the film

We wanted our wedding film to feel like a cinematic documentary, capturing the raw, honest, and unscripted moments of our day. Instead of having an SDE, we chose to create something closer to a music video, guided by a storyboard we prepared for our videography team. The song we chose was "God Was Showing Off" by Bruno Mars from The Romantic album because it perfectly described how we felt about the day. It felt like God truly showed off by giving us the best day of our lives, surrounded by the beauty of Batanes and the people we love.

What guests noticed (and didn't)

Our wedding was not just an event, but also an adventure. It was a long and tiring week, but seeing our family and friends experience the beauty of Batanes made it all worth it. Many were amazed by the breathtaking views, but the biggest surprise came during the reception when they saw the venue and the fireworks that closed the night. It was a celebration filled with beautiful moments, shared memories, and a place that left everyone in awe.

09 — What We'll Remember

Two voices, one wedding

Not a summary of the day, but the parts of it we know we'll carry with us.

Chris

“I still can’t quite believe we got to have all of this. It all began with a private post we shared on December 30, 2020, where we quietly manifested our dream wedding. At the time, only the two of us knew that dream existed. Years later, we found ourselves standing on that hillside in Batanes, watching it become our reality. Seeing her walk toward me with the whole island stretched out behind her is a moment I’ll replay for the rest of my life. But what made it even more special was sharing it with our families and taking them on the greatest adventure of our lives. Batanes didn’t just host our wedding. It became part of our story, where I married my best friend and where the dream only the two of us believed in finally came true.”

Batanes gave us the wedding we always dreamed of, and somehow, even more than that.”
